Chole Bhature

Description

A classic North Indian street food favorite featuring spicy, tangy chickpea curry paired with fluffy, deep-fried leavened bread. This hearty combination is a flavor explosion that serves as the perfect indulgent breakfast or weekend brunch.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Chickpeas (Kabuli Chana),
  • 2 tea bags (for traditional dark color)
  • 2 cups All-purpose flour (Maida)
  • 1/2 cup Semolina (Sooji)
  • 1/2 cup Yogurt (Curd)
  • 2 Onions, finely chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp Chole Masala spice blend
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix flour, semolina, salt, and yogurt in a bowl. Knead into a soft dough, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 2 hours.
  2. Cook the Chickpeas: Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with tea bags, salt, and water until soft (about 4-5 whistles). Discard tea bags.
  3. Make the Masala: Heat oil in a pan. Saut√© chopped onions until golden brown, then add ginger-garlic paste and tomato puree. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Simmer: Add the Chole Masala spices and the cooked chickpeas to the pan. Simmer for 15 minutes to let flavors absorb.
  5. Fry the Bhature: Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into flat circles. Deep fry in hot oil until they puff up and turn golden.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with pickled onions and green chili.
